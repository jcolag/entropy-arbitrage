## Carriotepunk

The book's page has the following description.

> Double récit sur le thème de l’errance, Carriotepunk est une nouvelle qui se promène sur des territoires post-capitalistes fictifs, où éoliennes à tambours et panneaux solaires alimentent les communautés qui ont fait de l’entraide une règle fondamentale. Un moment de lecture sous forme de voyage paisible, en suivant d’un côté le parcours d’un enfant recueilli sur la route, et en parallèle celui d’une jeune femme nomade qui a choisi d’aller se rendre utile de l’autre côté de la montagne, avec un chariot solaire pour bagage.
{: lang="fr" }

In English, that translates to this.

> Carriotepunk is a double story on the theme of wandering, and it is a new story that travels through fictional post-capitalist territories, where drum wind turbines and solar panels feed communities that have made mutual aid a fundamental rule. A moment of reading in the form of a peaceful journey, following on one side the journey of a child gathered on the road, and in parallel that of a young nomadic woman who chose to go to make herself useful on the other side of the mountain, with a solar cart for luggage.

I saw the author announce the first seven chapters in rapid succession in March, but it doesn't look like it has moved since then.  For now, then, we'll need to look at it as an introductory short story, and hope that the author hasn't forgotten the rest of the story.

Oh, and as usual, for the sake of expedience, I chose to read this via machine translation, so I can't guarantee that some nuance or depth won't get lost.

## What Works Well?

The story continues to expand the world without one of those "well, as you know..." expository scenes.

And maybe this has more to do with time invested than the writing, but it does feel like the characters have grown more distinct, rather than filling roles.

## What Works...Less Well?

{% include lesswell.md %}

We have one chapter that feels almost empty, despite about a thousand years.  The kid loosens up, which does count as progress, but the narrative uses a name for him, giving a weird sense that we've started watching a third set of characters rather than moving forward with one of the existing two groups.  In parallel, Amarille encounters a mysterious artifact that...she ignores, nothing to worry about, I guess.

I also don't love that the narrator seems central to the story, but somehow doesn't have a name, yet.

## Opportunities

I don't see anything, beyond leaving polite and encouraging comments.

## What's Adaptable?

I only noticed the Val Solas refuge station, which does seem like the most complicated place that we've seen.  Oh, I suppose that we also have the more abstract sense of how people navigate while traveling, communicating over the radio with personal call-signs to connect with those refuge stations.

## Six Years Hence:  The Floraverse:  Seeds

Continuing the look back at the early Free Culture Book Club posts, six years ago today, we started talking about [*Seeds*]({% post_url 2020-05-23-seeds %}), the introductory story for the [Floraverse](https://floraverse.com/), and the first of these that took up more than a single post.

I have to admit that, despite my enjoyment of the story and the supporting material, I haven't really kept up with it.  The Discord server expanded to multiple servers segregated by the fictional geography, which became focused almost entirely on free-form role-play, as in people primarily to exclusively posting as a character in that world, so I mostly muted them and moved on.  People who want to do that should have places to do that without people like me waiting for the meta-conversations and finding everything else completely opaque.

Another development, I forget the specific timeline assuming that I ever knew it, but around the time of those posts, the community recovered from a scandal in a way that leaders should probably study[^Lcu3Wi].  As I recall it, a prominent member of the community with a close relationship with the creator turned out to have abused other members of the community, using their position to shield the toxic relationships from scrutiny.  When discovered, the community came together to ban the offender despite their arguable authority, reached out to provide support to the victims coming forward, and had long and public conversations about how best to protect vulnerable community members in the future, restructuring some aspects to support that effort.

[^Lcu3Wi]:  Absolutely do **not** push your way into the community asking intrusive questions or soliciting a history, if you do want to investigate.  Approach the issue with as much sensitivity and least imposition as you can manage, since the incident did leave people hurt.

It didn't fix the trauma, as we can all guess, especially as the offender---predictably---took to social media with the stereotypical claims of victimhood by the imagined conspiracy of accusers.  But they prioritized protecting the community at a time when so many other groups instead protected their powerful people and gave their communities stories about needing to tend to their personal lives first, and might choose to engage with protecting the community when they have time...alongside the predictable claims of false accusations.

And despite those problems, and despite my lack of contact with the space, the work has definitely continued, with an animated short released as recently as November, and some games shortly before that.  And I still see it as some of the most interesting material that we've seen.
