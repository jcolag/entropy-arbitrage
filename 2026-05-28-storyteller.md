 > **O'BRIEN**: Oh, no, sir. Everything's running smoothly for a change. But around here, you never know.

Hence sending him to the surface, I imagine, so that they *can* know...

 > **BASHIR**: I see it as a wonderful opportunity for us to get to know each other.
 >
 > **O'BRIEN**: Ah.

It couldn't happen to a nicer guy, honestly...

OK, *really* honestly, a nicer guy would've pulled Bashir aside sometime over the past four months---stardate 46379 to 46729 gives us a difference of 350, and thirty-five percent of a year gives us a hundred twenty days, so literally four months, assuming that their seasons look like our years other than splitting them into a thousand dates---to tell him to tone it down...

 > **SISKO**: Relax, Major. This is not my first time up at the plate.

We get a quick reminder that Sisko likes baseball, as will the Jake-and-Nog subplot, surely casually, and not making sure that we keep it front-of-mind in two weeks.

 > **VARIS**: Commander Sisko?

You may recognize Varis as [Gina Philips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gina_Philips), not the biggest name that we've seen come through here, but has bumped around genre fiction and still gets work, which seems like almost as much of an achievement as becoming a big star or leaving the industry to do important work elsewhere.

 > **BASHIR**: Do I annoy you?

This whole dynamic seems bizarre.  I mean, you and I find Bashir abrasive and borderline worrying at this stage of the series.  But he and O'Brien come from pretty much the same mold, career Starfleet officers who see the universe in terms of colonial discoveries.  Had Bashir had a major recurring role on **The Next Generation**, for example, I don't think that he'd stand out at all, other than his relative youth.

 > **BASHIR**: What were you thinking about? I'm just curious.
 >
 > ...
 >
 > **BASHIR**: I don't think it's really necessary to call me sir.
 >
 > **O'BRIEN**: What should I call you? You're my superior officer.
 >
 > **BASHIR**: How about Julian?

Actually, scratch that.  Bashir might have stood out on the *Enterprise*, because this scene comes off as flirty, and we all know that a bisexual, especially a bisexual person of color, wouldn't have flown on **TNG**, where people barely come into physical contact except as part of an aggressive heterosexual overture.  But most of the other personality issues seem pretty normal on that side of the fence, such as what we'd read as sexual harassment[^l_29AG] if they made this more explicit flirting...

 > **FAREN**: Has he shown any sign of improvement?

He won't get a line for a while, but in the bed, you might recognize [Kay E. Kuter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kay_E._Kuter) playing the Sirah, known from minor roles pretty much everywhere for decades, but probably had his most regular gig as Newt Kiley, a recurring character between **Green Acres** and **Petticoat Junction**.  We heard his voice, at least, during [*The Nth Degree*]({% post_url 2024-03-14-nth-degree %}), as the creature pulling Barclay's strings.

 > **WOBAN**: I'll say this for the Cardassians. Their replicators make a fine larish pie.

And you might recognize Woban as [Jordan Lund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan_Lund), though usually a stage performer for most of his career, though we saw him briefly as one of the Klingons in [*Redemption*]({% post_url 2024-05-16-redemption-part-2 %}), and later plays a bounty hunter on **Enterprise**.

 > **HOVATH**: I'm here.

The last major guest, I believe, you might recognize Hovath as [Lawrence Monoson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lawrence_Monoson), who appeared quite a bit until about a decade ago, but he'll also show up on **Enterprise** as an unethical colleague.

Convenient for me that nothing interesting (except for that other baseball comment) happened between introducing most guests...

 > **O'BRIEN**: Strange. I'm not registering any atmospheric disturbance.

Why would he *check*, though, unless he read ahead in the script?  Wind doesn't seem remotely suspicious, after all, not even wind that starts at around the same time as some weird ceremony.

 > **O'BRIEN**: Bloody hell.

Rather than signaling the commercial break, do you maybe do something?  Help people, maybe?  No?  OK, to the commercials we go, I guess.  Remember this when they try to position him as some seasoned soldier who saved his platoon...

 > **VARIS**: I just spent the last five hours sitting.
 >
 > **SISKO**: Yes, five long, unproductive hours.

It seems pretty weird that Sisko apparently also sat around for five hours and has no problems continuing to do that.  He seems like the sort of person who would've suggested shifting to a walking meeting after the first hour...

 > **BASHIR**: You saw how he greeted you when we arrived. It was as if he were expecting you. I'll tell you this, Chief. I'm glad you came along on this mission, because if it wasn't for you, I'd have done what I could for the Sirah and left. And look at what I would have missed.

See?  That line could've come *right* out of a **TNG** episode, and everybody would've taken Riker or whoever seriously.

 > **BASHIR**: I mean, for all we know, you really were sent by the Prophets.
 >
 > **O'BRIEN**: I was sent by Commander Sisko. This Dal'Rok or whatever will be returning tonight and everybody's counting on me to be able to stop it. The only trouble is, I don't have the slightest idea how I'm supposed to do that.

The Prophets *did* name Sisko as their Emissary, so...

 > **FAREN**: These gifts are for you, Sirah.
 >
 > **O'BRIEN**: Well, tell them I don't want them.

When did the language barrier develop?  Last night, they all understood the brave and strong village squaring off against the greasy cloud.

 > **FAREN**: Is there a problem, Sirah?
 >
 > **O'BRIEN**: No! They're not...Well, yes, I mean, they're quite...Look, I have a wife and daughter back on DS Nine.

I didn't expect to get any mileage out of this plot, but O'Brien almost let himself inspect women like livestock, and only stopped because he realized that he needed to disengage.

 > **O'BRIEN**: Well, son, it looks like tonight you are going to get that chance.  Heh.

He *really* goes out of his way to dismiss...not even their beliefs, because it all has physical effects on everything, so he really dismisses them as people.

 > **O'BRIEN**: Let's get out of here before they change their minds.

And to top it off, he drops the cape on the ground instead of, like...putting it on Hovath's shoulders, maybe, to suggest some personal growth.

 > **BASHIR**: You know, Chief, this is going to make a fantastic story to tell around the station.

You know, it occurs to me that Bashir treats O'Brien in this episode the way that everybody else treats him, as a mildly amusing...I think that the kids use the term "hot mess," these days.  He starts out acting like he desperately wants a friend, but---and again, this feels like it recalls **TNG**---mostly seems to see O'Brien like a joke, probably the most endearing thing about the doctor so far...

By the way, it doesn't matter to us, but it occurs to me that the United States ratified [NAFTA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_American_Free_Trade_Agreement), bringing Mexico---which partly shares a river border on the [Rio Grande](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rio_Grande)---bringing the country into an updated version of the [Canada–United States Free Trade Agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada%E2%80%93United_States_Free_Trade_Agreement) later in the year this aired.  The treaty didn't adjust the border, but we all know that certain people like to keep that border as contentious as possible, and the plot about a contentious river border resolved with a free trade agreement does sound like NAFTA might have had some influence, here.

## Conclusions

We don't get much out of this, focusing as it does on specific Bajorans and O'Brien's stage fright.

### The Bad

Nobody on the crew has had a conversation with Bashir about his intrusiveness, apparently preferring to endure him silently than solve the problem.

In a crisis, O'Brien finds a quiet corner so that he can complain about it.  Maybe similarly, while Bashir definitely does his job, he also treats everybody around him like comedy sketches for him to appreciate.  And O'Brien needed a reminder of his marriage before stopping himself from evaluating women as property, and treats the Bajorans badly overall.

### The Weird

At least O'Brien appears to randomly check weather forecasts in hopes of contradicting what he can see.
