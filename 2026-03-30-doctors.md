## Mini-Server, Part 23

Having seen a flood of news about some popular book-management system or other falling apart under pressure from AI---or something along those lines, at least---it occurred to me to look at the state of the art out there, since I do have piles of e-books on the same hard drives as the video and music.  After some superficial research, I landed on [Kavita](https://www.kavitareader.com/).

I don't have much of a story, here.  The installation documentation worked perfectly.  It had problems with many of my files, which could happen for any number of reasons given some of their ages, and I can investigate some of them later.  But it works, and unlike Audio Bookshelf, it actually has the ability to show the books instead of only providing them for download.  It looks decent, otherwise.

I don't like that they want to sell a premium service---inevitably, this will mean pushing notifications into the application and changing to a proprietary license---but none of the premium features interest me, so I can ignore it until the scorpion turns on the John-shaped frog.
