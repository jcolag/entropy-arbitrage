## Vortex

For context, this episode would have followed [*Starship Mine*]({% post_url 2025-03-20-starship-mine %}) and [*Lessons*]({% post_url 2025-03-27-lessons %}), with no new **Next Generation** episode before next time.

 > **ODO**: He didn't seem to want to talk much to Sisko when he arrived.
 >
 > **QUARK**: Who would? All those Starfleet officers greeting him at the airlock. It would scare me too. The Federation could learn a few things from the Ferengi about hospitality.

Well, now I almost wish that the show had kept the running joke of the Starfleet crew all rushing down to yet another welcome ceremony every couple of weeks, only for the visitors to have no interest in talking to them, as we saw at the start of [*Move Along Home*]({% post_url 2026-04-30-move-along-home %})...

 > **RO-KEL**: One thousand bars of gold pressed latinum. Non-negotiable.

Probably not, but you might recognize both twins as [Randy Oglesby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randy_Oglesby), who we last saw as a "chorus" background player in [*Loud As a Whisper*]({% post_url 2022-12-22-loud-as-whisper %}), will show up again later in this series as an unrelated character, plus late in **Voyager**'s run, and two characters (one recurring) on **Enterprise**, among other appearances.

 > **CRODEN**: Just stay...stay...stay where you are, please. 

If Croden looks familiar, [Cliff DeYoung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cliff_DeYoung) plays him, lead singer of Clear Light, one of the leads in **Hair**, and appeared more in film than television.

 > **SISKO**: I want to be sure you understand exactly what's going to happen. There will be a trial to determine your guilt or innocence. We can assign an advocate, or you can request one from your home world.

I thought that this would at least hint at the shape of Federation civilian law, but Sisko later makes it clear that the Bajorans have jurisdiction.

 > **ODO**: A what?
 >
 > **CRODEN**: A changeling, like you.

They didn't mean this, and if they did, it certainly doesn't come through the performance and direction.  However, while I mostly giggle at the idea that Odo might hear the word "changeling" and immediately envision it as a proper noun and not literally a generic term (in speculative fiction, at least) for something that changes its shape---like Jake not understanding "Gratitude Festival" in [*The Nagus*]({% post_url 2026-05-07-nagus %})---Odo *could* have taken it as an insult.

The term, as most readers probably already know, comes from European folklore, the idea of a creature secretly swapped with a human baby for nefarious reasons, so it comes off a bit like a bastard, in its literal sense.  Stronger in some senses, by extension, it has also sometimes---mostly the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, as far as I can tell---meant something like a simpleton, imagining those magical critters having difficulty understanding the human world.

 > **DAX**: It won't be hard to find Rakhar. According to the logs, Croden was found in a damaged shuttle-craft about three light years from the wormhole. There aren't many M-class planets in that area.

Maybe a silly question, but months in, why hasn't the Federation made contact with every planet near the wormhole?  They keep making a big deal about these first contacts, and introducing yourself to the new neighbors only seems polite.

 > **CRODEN**: I'm sorry if I misled you. There were shape-shifters on Rakhar at one time, but that was centuries ago.
 >
 > **ODO**: What happened to them?
 >
 > **CRODEN**: They were persecuted. Driven out. But I know a place where there are still some left. Of course, that was a few years ago.

Not relevant here, but we'll find out that the actual Gamma Quadrant Changelings do have something like this history, and it seems like a shame that the show/franchise never bothered to follow up on that.

 > **HADRAN**: I am the Exarch of Nehelik Province. You will transport Croden to this location.

You might recognize [Gordon Clapp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gordon_Clapp) playing Hadran, autumn of that year to play Medavoy for the next dozen years on **NYPD Blue**, but you've also probably seen him *somewhere* else.

 > **HADRAN**: I do not know what kind of people you are. I do not care to know. But if your society purports to respect the rules of our society, you will return him without delay.
 >
 > **SISKO**: Expect him to return in a vessel just like this one within fifty-two hours.

It seems worth a note that this episode broadly falls between [*Chain of Command*]({% post_url 2025-01-30-chain-command-2 %}) where Riker advocated for invading Cardassian space to break Picard out of prison and [*Frame of Mind*]({% post_url 2025-04-10-frame-mind %}) where Picard decided that he could do whatever he wanted on a planet because it didn't have a unified government, two incidents in a long tradition of *Enterprise*-D officers deciding that they didn't need to respect the local laws.  By contrast, Sisko tries to object, or at least gather more information, and ultimately decides to respect the decision.

He doesn't *like* the outcome, but his demeanor suggests more of a disappointment that the conversation didn't go better, and he'll need to sacrifice his visitor more than that they pulled rank.

 > **AH-KEL**: The coordinates are very clearly stated right here in the log entry. One more thing, little Ferengi. You better be telling the truth, or I'll be back to finish this.

For all the times that this crew and other Federation people bash the Ferengi for not caring about anybody, it does seem worth pointing out that Quark tried to protect Odo and Croden, here, and almost got himself killed doing it.  It recalls [*Suspicions*]({% post_url 2025-04-17-suspicions %}) in how it shows the bias against the entire culture despite it not matching up to how they actually operate.

 > **COMPUTER**: A temporary loss of stability resulting from the impact of a concentrated plasma charge.

How do they have worse computers than we...had then, I guess, since we've since "caught up" to computers that can now waste time by answering primarily with the part that the questioner already knew, "a temporary loss of stability," in this case.  Odo needs to ask *two more* questions, too, to get the actual answer of "a Miradorn vessel fired on us."  But I guess it did maximize engagement, so...

 > **VULCAN**: Starfleet runabout, this is the Vulcan science vessel *T'Vran*. Are you in any difficulty?

You might recognize the captain, to the extent that she appears, as [Kathleen Garrett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kathleen_Garrett), who mostly shows up as a character actor, but did feature through about half of the first season of **The New WKRP in Cincinnati**.

## Conclusions

We get more information about the nebula than the Federation, so this might not take long...

### The Good

Sisko, unlike (apparently many) others in Starfleet, accepts when foreign governments have priorities that don't overlap with the Federation's.

### The Bad

We see fairly openly, once again, that the anti-Ferengi stereotypes don't rise much higher than propaganda.

Computers continue to have the worst user interfaces, almost actively avoiding answering direct questions.

### The Weird

Despite the alleged excitement of encountering "new life and new civilizations," to paraphrase somebody, the Federation seems entirely disinterested in reaching out to the cultures on the other side of the wormhole, even acknowledging that only a few such planets even exist nearby.
