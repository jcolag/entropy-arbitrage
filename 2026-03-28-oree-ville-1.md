I provide it to help you make a decision, rather than a decision in and of itself.

## À l’orée de la ville

The primary page for the book describes it as follows.

> Professionnel de l’usurpation d’identité grâce à Civi, son assistant IA, Füerzò est un pirhack. Grâce à ses compétences, il prospère en détournant tout ce qui lui est nécessaire dans un monde où la misère sociale est exacerbée par des monnaies inégalitaires et une surveillance automatisée et absurde. Déconnecté de cette sombre réalité, Füerzò reçoit une mission dans un hôpital qui va changer sa vie et sa vision du monde. Quelles alternatives s’offrent à un pirhack lorsqu’il est arraché à son hyperconnexion ?

In English, that says the following.

> A professional identity thief, thanks to Civi (his AI assistant), Füerzò works as a pirate. Thanks to his skills, he thrives by diverting everything he needs in a world where unequal currencies and automated excessive surveillance exacerbate social misery. Disconnected from this dark reality, Füerzò receives a mission in a hospital that will change his life and his vision of the world. What alternatives does a pirate have, when he finds himself torn from his hyper-connection?

And for context, we even get an author bio, this time.

> En pleine reconversion professionnelle après deux ans au service du CNRS en tant que chercheur en géologie, Allius a profité de son récent temps libre pour concrétiser une de ses passions de longue date : l’écriture. Mordu de fantasy et de science-fiction, c’est naturellement qu’il se tourne vers le dernier genre dans lequel il peut exprimer tout son engagement à travers son premier texte publié : À l’orée de la ville.

Again, we can look at it in English.

> In full professional conversion after two years serving the CNRS as a researcher in geology, Allius took advantage of his recent free time to realize one of his long-standing passions: writing. Fantasy and science fiction enthusiast, it feels natural that he turns to the latter genre in which he can express all his commitment through his first published text: *At the edge of the city*.

We don't generally get background like this, so bravo!

## What Works Well?

Without much overt exposition, we get one of the most fully realized worlds that I think that we've seen.  Not too long into the book, you get a fairly clear idea of how most people live and how our protagonist differs from the norm.

We also get a couple of funny moments scattered around.

## What Works...Less Well?

{% include lesswell.md %}

The story often feels abstract to me.  We get a build-up to an event, going through the preparation, but then we skip over the activity with some yeah-it-happened hand-waving.  At the end of this section, it instead tries to build tension during the action, but then tells us that something could have happened, but nah.

## What's Adaptable?

I'd call the world itself the big draw, here, a near-future where the non-wealthy volunteer for organizations in exchange for "points" that allow them to buy respites from the relentless corporate nickel-and-diming.  However, we also get a mention of [Ambroise Paré](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambroise_Par%C3%A9) Center.  It looks like the university and defense contractor both do exist, however.
