## Dramatis Personae

For context, this episode came out after [*Second Chances*]({% post_url 2025-05-01-second-chances %}), with no **TNG** episode between this and the next **DS9** episode two weeks later.

And this episode might not provide us with much, since I remember it focused almost entirely on the plot.

 > **SISKO**: It will if the Valerians want to continue trading with the Federation.

Notice that Sisko uses the flip-side of what we often saw on **The Next Generation**, where they constantly made trade deals with shady governments, with the implication that the Federation needed them for whatever reason.  Sisko at least seems to think that, because of the Federation's size, they represent something close to a [monopsony](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/monopsony), and so can---occasionally, since nobody in Kira's orbit has raised concerns about the Federation potentially walking away from Bajor---demand a change in government policies.

You might think that this sounds naïve, and it does sometimes fall flat on its face.  But if you look through history, tariffs have generally succeeded in one of two mutually exclusive things.  First, as we saw through most of last year, it funnels bribes to powerful people in exchange for better rates or carve-outs for their particular products.  Second, tariffs have successfully forced political change by turning the industrial sector of a country against its government.  They have never driven down prices, however, almost by definition.

 > **O'BRIEN**: Imagine taking eleven bored schoolkids on a visit to the grain processing center at Lasuma. I'm glad it's not me.

You know, it hadn't occurred to me until now, but while the Starfleet people (except for Sisko, more often than not) generally come out of the same mold, they do differ distinctly from what we saw on the *Enterprise*-D in that they all (including Sisko) express enthusiasm for something.  Bashir's enthusiasm tends towards the colonial and misogynist, sure, but you can't claim that he doesn't get excited.

O'Brien, though, has that same adolescent detachment that the *Enterprise* crew loves, thinking that it makes him cool to not enjoy anything.

And appropriately, as fans of the show already know and if you'll pardon my hopping around the timeline, when he does find things to get passionate about, they all have at least a childish veneer to them, such as getting together with Bashir to play with ~~dolls~~ ~~*action figures*~~ *totally serious war simulations*.

 > **O'BRIEN**: Victory during a bio-survey?

See what I mean?  Sometimes you need to make your own fun, but he doesn't see it.  He'd never consider finishing work early a win...

 > **KIRA**: They just visited Fahleena Three and Mariah Four, the same two stops they used to make when they were running dolamide to the Cardassians. The last stop would be the purification plant on Ultima Thule. Now if I can place them there, we'll know they're running weapons-grade material.

They probably made up the first two names, but the Ancient Greeks and Romans used the name [Thule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thule) for [Marseille](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marseille), but when southern France stopped seeming exotic, it drifted its way to an imagined island north of the British Isles, maybe Iceland, and Ultima Thule (by extension) probably Greenland.  The Nazis, constantly obsessed with the Roman Empire, decided that their imaginary[^MkcEsa] Aryan people came from the not-French Thule, creating entire half-assed secret societies about how their imaginary heritage gave them access to magic.  You know, totally normal stuff that doesn't expose white supremacy as desperation to feel important in the grand scheme at all.

[^MkcEsa]:  Unless you mean Iranians, which probably does account for the origin of the term, but Nazis generally don't want to hear about that sort of thing.

Anyway, long story short, because of the association of Thule with "way the Hell north" and "Ultima Thule" with "even further than that," this doesn't say so explicitly, but it sounds like the name might replace [Polaris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polaris), both because Earth's poles don't make much different in space, and because [Earth's precession](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axial_precession) means that the pole has already started drifting away from Polaris and towards [Gamma Cephei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamma_Cephei) (Errai), which will get the title in around a (our time) thousand years, of which this show takes place a good chunk of the way along.

And yes, a thousand years would land roughly when **Starfleet Academy** takes place.

 > **QUARK**: This crew was worse than the last. Our glorious mission. I said, what's so glorious about a gamma quadrant bio-survey?

Again, see?  The Klingons enjoy their work, Miles...

 > **BASHIR**: Commander Sisko and Kira. Mark my words, there's going to be trouble.

I wonder if this idea comes out of the "secret" story, or if people see Sisko and Kira's working relationship as something other than the respect and support with occasional disagreements that *we* see.

 > **SISKO**: Commanded by a Federation officer. Commanded by me. Are you challenging that, Major?

That does feel like something that she *could* challenge, no?  Again, they've made it clear that we shouldn't take any characters too seriously in this episode, but the Federation commands the station at the request of the Bajoran provisional government, and they can revoke that request.

 > **O'BRIEN**: Well, I wouldn't get too friendly with the natives if I were you. Remember where your loyalties lie.

Funny how you can barely tell whether O'Brien got infected with any of this, since he speaks like this normally.  Throw in a "bloody'ell," and I'd assume that something made him immune...

 > **DAX**: You know what they say, put the shoe on the right foot first, but put the left foot first into the bathtub.

Compare this to the "spider under the table" hand-wringing in [*Lower Decks*]({% post_url 2025-09-04-lower-decks %}).  That metaphor seemed crystal clear.  This, though, presumably means...something?

 > **BASHIR**: Do you think we could use this to our advantage?

This episode needed Garak...

 > **SISKO**: You know what disturbs me? The ingratitude. I offered you my kindness, my help, my leadership, and how do you repay me? With betrayal. But you won't get rid of me so easily. You see, unlike you, I understand history. My name will blaze across the stars long after your petty treacheries have been forgotten.

THANK YOU FOR YOUR ATTENTION TO THIS MATTER.

...Sorry, but the episode feels far less alien when we have people ranting like this on a regular basis and getting news coverage for it.  Please nobody archive our dimwits onto telepathic spheres.

## Conclusions

As expected, we didn't get much out of this, especially given how many lines don't necessarily reflect the characters speaking them.

### The Good

While apparently rare, the Federation does seem to pressure allied governments economically into enacting better policies.

### The Bad

O'Brien exemplifies the lack of enthusiasm that we've seen elsewhere, making a point to tell us how much he would hate to learn about grain processing with a class of students or surveying planets for potential colonization.
