Today, the Chinese-influenced parts of the world celebrate variations on the [Qingming Festival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qingming_Festival), celebrating spring fifteen days after the equinox.  Celebrants clean family graves and leave offerings for ancestors, analogous to [Radonitsa or Provody](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radonitsa) in Slavic communities (this or next week, depending on the church).

![Qingming Festival, Chonghe Dong Cemetery, Kolkata](/blog/assets/Qingming-festival.png "How did the rest of the world not catch onto holidays where you celebrate by cleaning public spaces?")

And on to the week's projects.

## Entropy Arbitrage, the Code

{% codeberg jcolag/entropy-arbitrage-code %}

You should see three small changes peeking around the edges of the blog.

First, and maybe most important, charts now have "fallback" descriptions for cases where you wouldn't see the chart itself, such as visual impairments, JavaScript turned off, RSS, or whatnot.  It still requires me to *write* the description, so brace yourself on those occasional posts for attempts to explain what you would see, beyond "graph of function f(x) = whatever."

Then, something made me validate the style sheets, leading me to spot a handful of typos, mostly errant hyphens and one extra comma in a list.  Browsers generally do a good job of guessing the intent within reason, but it makes more sense to have these things clean.

Finally, for an upcoming post---because they don't get used around here often---links with an image as the "text" won't have an annotation icon, since that looks incredibly ugly.  That seems confusing.  Let's look at an example, a variation on the header image.

[![Qingming Festival, Chonghe Dong Cemetery, Kolkata](/blog/assets/Qingming-festival.png "Click to see the source on Wikimedia Commons")](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Chonghe_Dong_Cemetery,_Qingming_Festival_4.jpg)

Yes, this repeats the header image that you might still have on the screen, *but* it links to where I got the image, so you can click through to Wikimedia Commons.  Prior to this change, that would have had the WC logo floating after it, which doesn't make much sense for images.

## Ham Newsletter

{% codeberg jcolag/ham-newsletter %}

With no other reasonable place to put it, because it sits on the same infrastructure, the newsletter-generation repository now includes the code that I use to plan the [social media roundup](/blog/tag/link-dump) posts and schedule the morning and noon toots on Mastodon.  It works the same way as the existing script, used at the end of the month to grab bookmarks from [Shiori](https://github.com/go-shiori/shiori) and render them to Markdown, *but* it checks my list of Fediverse handles used by my sources and uses that (along with a clearer name) when identifying the link.

Even though it took me a few years to figure this out, as I have said before, this encourages readers to engage with the outlet (or individual blogger), encouraging them to stay on non-corporate spaces.  On the down-side, it reads as if I want their attention, so a few of them have taken an awkward moment to thank me...
