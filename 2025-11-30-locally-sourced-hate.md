## Not Who We Are

Sure, countries such as Russia do often [exploit existing polarization {% cc %}](https://www.ft.com/content/b9ab01f9-e654-44c8-a562-bd97630cab5e) in hopes of weakening the obstacles to expanding their influence.  But they can't exactly exploit existing polarization if that polarization doesn't...well, *exist*.

Let's not forget that every single country in the Western Hemisphere exists because an imperial power ignored native populations to claim that they "discovered" the place, then took on ethnic cleansing and/or forced labor campaigns against those natives who somehow didn't know about the local continents before the invaders.  In the United States, we expanded on that by inventing chattel slavery.  The entire world has had different forms of slavery, and the whole Western Hemisphere engaged in plantation systems, but Americans uniquely decided to own and raise humans as livestock for centuries of wage theft.  We fought a war over whether to expand that system west (as we drove the native populations off the land that we promised to leave alone for them), and when the war put an end to that and the practice of chattel slavery, we spent about ten years trying to fix the underlying problems and then shrugged our shoulders, so that we could squeeze out another century of pretending that slavery still existed.

Heck, the entire world has had thousands of years of misogyny as a central, load-bearing societal structure.  You don't even need to go further back in history than two hundred fifty years to reach a world where democracy didn't exist in any meaningful form (almost) *anywhere*.

And going local again, as I mentioned in the post about dystopia stories, we had plenty of leaders such as [Henry Ford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Ford) and [Prescott Bush](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prescott_Bush) who supported the Nazis, and their kind [tried to overthrow democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_Plot).  None of that even tries to get to the part where we [denied Asians entry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Exclusion_Act) to the country for decades, including falsely [accusing them of sex work and other crimes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Page_Act_of_1875).  Or even in my lifetime, we could talk about the time that police actually [bombed a residential neighborhood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1985_MOVE_bombing), because the city escalated from noise and sanitation complaints, and we watched that story vanish from the news after a few days.

Seriously, I could go on for a *long* time, here, between atrocities that we inherited as we colonized North America and those atrocities that we committed for ourselves.  Every country does.

Speaking as somebody who loves this country, we can't pretend that every problem comes from mysterious foreign influences.  Every country has inherited and committed atrocities, but only healthy nations deal with them.  Early on in the blog's run, I referred to making this choice as [Turd Theory]({% post_url 2020-03-01-electability %}), where responsible people clean soiled surfaces before worrying about blame.  Or as [G.K. Chesterton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G._K._Chesterton) put it in [*A Defense of Patriotism*](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Defendant/A_Defence_of_Patriotism)...

> "My country, right or wrong," is a thing that no patriot would think of saying except in a desperate case.  It is like saying, "My mother, drunk or sober."  No doubt if a decent man's mother took to drink he would share her troubles to the last; but to talk as if he would be in a state of gay indifference as to whether his mother took to drink or not is certainly not the language of men who know the great mystery.

We need to come to terms with our own history and clean house, so that we don't need to keep slogging through this garbage.  When given the choice, we frequently opt for, as bell hooks put it, imperial white supremacist patriarchy.  We opt otherwise more often than a lot of cultures have and do---hence my loving the country---but we still have a lot of work to do to sober up.

## Recap

If you didn't get the point earlier, let's review the country's historical record in the context of how people talk about alleged foreign interference.

- "Russian-style disinformation," as mentioned, derives directly from the [Big Tobacco playbook](https://theconversation.com/smoking-rates-in-us-have-fallen-to-all-time-low-but-how-did-they-ever-get-so-high-107185).  And note that Big Tobacco literally grew out of an economy built on [forced labor camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plantation_complexes_in_the_Southern_United_States), and pivoted quickly from exploiting the labor of Black people to [destroying their bodies](https://web.archive.org/web/20210412180223/https://www.commondreams.org/views/2021/04/12/racist-roots-big-tobacco) with the product.
- The Nazis [modeled the Nuremberg Laws on Jim Crow](https://blackamericaweb.com/2025/04/07/ged-section-lessons-unlearned-how-jim-crow-inspired-nazi-germany/), studying the treatment of ex-slaves as a path to excluding Jewish people from society "politely" enough that the international community would ignore it.
- Speaking of Nazis, we treat concentration camps like "a German thing," despite our [concentration camps for Japanese Americans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internment_of_Japanese_Americans) at around the same time and [mass incarceration creating labor camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penal_labor_in_the_United_States).
- Religious fundamentalism, far from coming to our shores from the Taliban or whatever other Muslim group frightens conservatives this week, [Puritans established much of New England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_Puritans_in_North_America), and you can draw a direct line from them to the so-called [Moral Majority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moral_Majority) (neither moral nor a majority).
- We talk about [Russian oligarchs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_oligarchs) influencing the [broligarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broligarchy), as if we didn't perfect the [Gilded Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gilded_Age), with exactly that same combination of conspicuous consumption among the wealthy, disinterest in the fate of the poor, political corruption, and so forth.
- [Political correctness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_correctness) and [cancel culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cancel_culture) try to frame a government enforcing a viewpoint---usually fictionally, in modern discourse, because the terms mysteriously vanish, when conservatives actually ban books and fire people for [improper ideology {% cc %}](https://www.vanityfair.com/news/story/trump-improper-ideology-order-smithsonian)---as coming from someplace like Russia or China, as if we didn't have *two* [Red Scares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Red_Scare) in the twentieth century with the deliberate aim of systematically purging any forces that might impede capitalistic exploitation.
- Even so-called [Hungarian-style autocracy {% cc %}](https://apnews.com/article/trump-orban-hungary-autocracy-authoritarian-republicans-dfdf6299a614ec4e364be37c1132e446) sounds exactly like the [Second Red Scare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Scare#Second_Red_Scare_%281947%E2%80%931957%29) and the behavior of the United States during the Cold War in general, not only trying to excise any thinking opposed to the military-industrial complex, but propping up tin-pot dictators in [banana republics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banana_republic)[^wXo_rM] as alleged obstacles to the [spread of Communism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domino_theory).

## Warning Bells

And with that all said, if we whitewash global and American history, then we leave ourselves vulnerable to the next propaganda campaign, because believing that we don't have those kinds of problems here---as the [1776 Commission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1776_Commission)[^Dx_sUL] would've insisted, you might recall, and successor initiatives still insist---means that our resistance to our worst impulses atrophies.  After all, why do you think that *they* want to whitewash history?  Why do you think that so much conservative thought has turned to never making straight white men think about inequality, or why they associate patriotism with ignoring the uncomfortable parts of history?

[^Dx_sUL]:  I read the 1776 Commission report when it came out with the intent of disassembling it on the blog, but it didn't have anything bad in an *interesting* way, so I abandoned the idea...

Don't think of this risk as hypothetical or futuristic, by the way.  Think about how often criticisms of the Trump minions drift into offensive stereotypes or vague insinuations[^7lwZDd] instead of professional incompetence, for example.  The fact that the latter applies doesn't make the former acceptable.  But if we haven't learned how our predecessors might have associated the two, then we can only avoid it accidentally.

[^7lwZDd]:  For a painfully common example across independent media, think about how often you'll hear someone working themselves up into a lather over some conservative's sexual indiscretion or sexuality, whether soliciting sex workers or having non-heterosexual affairs, then rush to cover their lapse by explaining that they don't *actually* care about the sex part---even though they attacked it specifically, multiple times, and mentioned nothing else---but their *hypocrisy*...which they didn't mention at all until they realized that they have a reputation to protect, and which we've found doesn't work against a social class that doesn't care about shame.  And compare that process to the process of stochastic terrorism, where a powerful figure incites hate against a disadvantaged group and then insist that they don't support violence when violence directly results from those words.

This has loomed for a while, since long before I started bringing it up.  Consider all the times that you've heard Trump himself referred to as the puppet of some foreign power.  While that may ultimately prove true, it seems reckless to assume that when the more likely possibility exists that he "only" hates the country's people, laws, traditions, and ideals.  We all know that "real estate fan fiction"---loving the country but hating everything about it except for the actual land---has become a staple of conservative patriotism.

Remember:  We don't need to import fancy foreign hate.  We already have plenty of home-grown hate to go around, too much even, so don't ignore it.  Maybe we can even consider cleaning some of it up, remediating the country like a cultural Superfund site.
