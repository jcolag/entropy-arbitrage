I said this before in my post on the [Software Crisis]({% post_url 2026-03-29-software-crisis %}), but I admittedly take a certain perverse delight in thinking about how much it'll cost all the (surviving) companies to recover from their AI binges.

Consider that many embraced these tools primarily because they saw tech workers as gaining too much power over employers.  They've used it to excuse cutting the workforce, while burning their reputations *and* gutting their institutional knowledge.  As the AI-bubble starts to spring leaks, they'll need a bigger staff, probably at higher wages, and you might even see unions form, with everybody seeing that the industry doesn't value labor and experience.

And as the article points out, these LLLM price costs will come out of nowhere, meaning that these companies will need to make those decisions in a panic, too...
