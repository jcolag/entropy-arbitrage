## Mundo Virtual

The album describes itself as follows.

> Compuesto y grabado entre Marzo del 2020 y Marzo del 2021
>
> Excepto Fiesta Lan grabado en Noviembre del 2019
>
> Todo grabado y mezclado en un dormitorio en San Miguel, Buenos Aires por Juani A. 

In English, that comes to this.

> Composed and recorded between March 2020 and March 2021
>
> Except party Lan recorded in November 2019
>
> All engraving and mixed in a bedroom in San Miguel, Buenos Aires by Juani A.

The band, likewise, describes itself as follows.

> WWW es un cuarteto retro-futurista de punk internetdependiente de Buenos Aires, Argentina.

Again, in English.

> WWW is a retro-futuristic, Internet-dependent punk quartet in Buenos Aires, Argentina.

That doesn't give us much to go on, admittedly.  On the other hand, we also get a fairly short album.

## What Works Well?

The music strikes me as well-written and well-produced.  I wouldn't call it to my taste, but I *think* that if you've ever wanted punk music that sounds like a chip-tune, then you'll probably at least find this interesting.

Likewise, it does have some clever bits.  Let's take an example.

> Occupy all the online spaces  
> Infect society with the virus
>
> The black virus  
> Digital anarchy  
> is spreading  
> more and more every day

Long-time readers know that I don't see anarchy as workable any more than I enjoy chip-tunes, nor do I see the Internet becoming less authoritarian, *but* the concept fits thematically.

Finally, because I have commented about the opposite with other music, I should note that I appreciate that the songs center the lyrics.  We have had albums where five-or-more-minute-long songs had maybe a couple of dozen words.  Here, other than one instrumental piece, you don't get long stretches of relative nothing.

## What Works...Less Well?

{% include lesswell.md %}

The album seems indecisive about its own concept, at least in lyrics.  Everything about it bills itself as punk, but you really only get that from *Realidad Virtual* and *Virus Negro Digital*.  At the risk of sounding like an old man, by contrast, *Fiesta LAN* and *Placer Artificial* feel like the sort of glee in mainstream media that punks generally laugh at.

## Opportunities

You can buy the album (pay what you want) and its six siblings.

## What's Adaptable?

I didn't catch anything, unless you count the aforementioned "Black Digital Virus" spreading anarchy.
