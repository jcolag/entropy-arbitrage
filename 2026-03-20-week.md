<i class="fas fa-square"></i> [Jay Graber Steps Down as Bluesky CEO](https://wedistribute.org/2026/03/jay-graber-steps-down-as-bluesky-ceo/) from We Distribute

> For now, Bluesky’s biggest obstacle is growth. As of this month, Bluesky has over an estimated 40 million user accounts, but that’s still a drop in the bucket compared to big social networks such as X (roughly 550 million amounts) and Facebook (3.07 billion users). Add in Bluesky’s aspirations to provide a network that goes far beyond microblogging, and the picture of what Bluesky needs is much clearer.

I have admittedly wanted an excuse to comment on this for a while, so even though I don't buy into the "really an engineer" explanation, it'll do.

Given that I don't interact with Bluesky, I only have external evidence, but this gives me the impression *not* of failure (don't think for a minute that anybody in charge cares about her rants against moderation) and not of needing someone who has experience with larger customer bases.  Rather, especially with "a partner at VC firm" as interim CEO, this looks like the endgame.  Presumably, this means either ads to start pulling as much value out of the network as possible or algorithmic content right on time for the midterm elections in the United States---see the earlier article about {% x %}'s algorithms pushing people to right-wing content---for the usual reasons, possibly both.

What did everybody expect?  They created the project at Twitter, to head off the Fediverse's increasing popularity, and made it look like Twitter.  The idea that it'll follow Twitter's evolution shouldn't sound too unlikely.  We'll need to see if independent servers such as Blacksky have enough independence that they can stay in the network while protecting their users.
