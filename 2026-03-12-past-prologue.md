## Disclaimer

In these posts, we discuss a non-"Free as in Freedom" popular culture franchise property, including occasional references to part of that franchise behind a paywall. ## Previously...

I initially outlined the project [in this post]({% post_url 2020-01-02-trek-00 %}), for those falling into this from somewhere else.  In short, we attempt to use the details presented in *Star Trek* to assemble a view of what life looks like in the Federation.  This "phase" of the project changes from previous posts, once again.  **Deep Space Nine** takes place long after the original series and contemporary with/following **The Next Generation**, so we shouldn't expect identical politics and socialization.  Maybe more importantly, I enjoy the series more.

In plain language, you shouldn't read this expecting a recap or review of an episode.  Many people have done both endlessly over nearly sixty years.  You *will* find a catalog of information that we learn from each episode, though, so expect everything to potentially "spoil" a story for a series that ended decades ago.

Rather than list every post in the series here, you can quickly find them all on [the *Star Trek* tag page](/blog/tag/star-trek/).

## Past Prologue

Since last time, nothing happened, because **The Next Generation** continues.

Actually, something did happen, technically, because I somehow got the episode order wrong.  If you pay careful attention to the *Stay Tuned* sections, then you might recall that I announced that this episode would cover *A Man Alone*.  I assume that I ran into some sort of off-by-one error, overlooking this hour.

Oh, and not really relevant, but since they introduce the things in this episode and fans often find it yet another exotic idea that they must have made up, the term *runabout* refers to an agile powered boat that holds four-to-eight passengers.  Given the implication---soon to quietly fall by the wayside, honestly---that they mostly use these in-system, you'd probably most closely compare them to bow riders, the almost stereotypical recreational boat that often gets showcased for waterskiing and similar sports.

 > **GARAK**: It's Doctor Bashir, isn't it? Of course it is. May I introduce myself?

It hadn't occurred to me that Garak appeared this early in the series, and you probably recognize him "because Garak," *but* [Andrew Robinson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_Robinson_%28actor%29) has shown up pretty much everywhere for decades, most prominently (other than this role) in **Dirty Harry**.  He also played Liberace, which couldn't *possibly* have informed any of the decisions about this character.

 > **BASHIR**: Er, yes, yes, of course.

Shocked that anybody would voluntarily speak to him, I see.

 > **BASHIR**: You know, some people say that you remained on DS Nine as the eyes and ears of your fellow Cardassians.

The Federation embraces all people, especially if they can accuse them and their entire cultures of nefarious dealings, I guess.

 > **BASHIR**: You'll never believe who just sat down next to me at the Replimat.

I've never seen anybody so giddy about meeting somebody.  I almost reached for {% emoji musical notes %} *Tell me more, tell me more, like does he have a car?* {% emoji musical notes %} but that comes off as disapproving, when this quickly becomes one of the franchise's healthiest ~~romances~~ *totally platonic male friendships* that couldn't ever offend any executives[^flRkV5].

[^flRkV5]:  I vaguely remember hearing that the franchise employed a minion who insisted that Gene Roddenberry would never have accepted any non-heterosexual romances in his intellectual property and so enforced that imaginary rule, apparently never noticing the frequent hints at a Kirk-Spock relationship or how often Roddenberry himself joked that, after filming *The Cage*, he could have kept Barrett as Number One and married Leonard Nimoy.

Seriously, just ask him out, doc.

I admittedly haven't investigated---I barely have time for the actual franchise, let alone any extensions---but for those who have the time, I gather that, during pandemic lockdowns, Robinson and (now professionally known as) Alexander Siddig took to streaming dramatic readings of fan fiction about their characters.

 > **BASHIR**: In fact, Chief O'Brien, I think you should place a monitoring device on me. Well, just in case he's up to something?

A monitoring device, such as...his badge?

 > **TAHNA**: My name is Tahna Los. Request political asylum. Kira?

Unrelated to the line except for the timing, going into the credits reminds me of something that I forgot to mention a couple of weeks ago:  This show finally tries to unify its "trade dress" with the then-current films, using a typeface (or two) that actually match fairly well to create a cohesive "brand," instead of **The Next Generation**'s insistence on using an entirely different typeface, a commercial font (Crillee) first release in 1980, despite modifying **The Motion Picture**'s main theme as its own.

Oh, actually somebody probably also wants to know that [Jeffrey Nordling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeffrey_Nordling) (no, not Combs) plays Tahna, though he still has most of his recognizable roles ahead of him at this point.

 > **SISKO**: I suggest you get your priorities straight, Major, because I don't have any room for divided loyalties in this command. And I won't have a Kohn-Ma terrorist using this station as a protective shield for continuing violence.

The second part, I get, but for the divided loyalties...he *requested* that, no?  He requested a Bajoran national as his second-in-command, and therefore somebody not beholden to Starfleet.  Did he want her there to boss her around?

 > **ROLLMAN**: Thank you for bringing this to my attention, Major. I'll stay in close touch with the situation.

Ready for this one?  [Susan Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susan_Bay) plays the admiral, here, who appeared a handful of times over the years, but people know her now as more of a director and political activist, plus ex-wife of John Schuck---the Klingon ambassador in [**The Voyage Home**]({% post_url 2022-04-07-tvh %}) and [**The Undiscovered Country**]({% post_url 2022-04-21-tuc %}) among more than a few other roles including a few more ahead of us---Leonard Nimoy's widow, and Michael Bay's cousin.  Good people, from what I can tell, in other words.

 > **O'BRIEN**: Sir, I don't know much about the Bajoran terrorists, but you've never fought the Cardassians, have you?

Note the same tone as asking if he ever worked with a Bajoran woman in [*Emissary*]({% post_url 2026-02-26-emissary-1 %}).

 > **SISKO**: Danar, in war, both sides commit atrocities.

Well, that sounds fairly damning, considering that the Federation apparently fought a war against the Federation, and we've seen Picard under normal circumstances.  If Sisko can shrug off war crimes and terrorism on a "both sides" basis, then he has seen more than a bit of it.

 > **LURSA**: Klingons do not surrender their weapons.

We last saw the Klingon sisters with Data deploying his new "emotions" to cheer their deaths in [**Generations**]({% post_url 2025-12-11-star-trek-generations-2 %}), but I believe that chronologically, they would have last appeared in [*Firstborn*]({% post_url 2025-10-16-firstborn %}).

 > **SISKO**: They tried to grab control of the Klingon High Council, started a brief civil war. They've been out of sight since then.

OK, [*Redemption*]({% post_url 2024-05-16-redemption-part-2 %}), then.  Thanks, Commander...

 > **ODO**: If they're enemies of the Klingon High Council?
 >
 > **SISKO**: They haven't broken any laws here. You can't just throw them in jail.

Can't they, though?  They still have some political alliance with the Empire, so I would imagine that they have some legal obligation to at least notify the government.  A friendly government wants them arrested for crimes.  I wouldn't *recommend* the action, because handing dissidents over to their governments seems like a terrible policy, but that wouldn't make it less of an obligation or less of an option.

Maybe we don't actually know the political situation with the Klingons; even when they occasionally talked about it as an alliance, we never really had a handle on anybody's responsibilities or benefits, I guess.  And I suppose that Bajor probably doesn't have any relationship with the Klingons, and their laws matter most.

 > **B'ETOR**: Good. Because if we have made this trip for nothing, you will have made a fatal mistake.

Wait...Did they have this conversation for Odo's benefit?  I don't see any other reason to congregate and shout about it.

 > **SISKO**: Go over my head again, and I'll have *yours* on a platter.

This seems inappropriate, but I think that, more than any actual offense to Sisko---after all, had Kira gone to her superiors, who then contacted Starfleet, I don't think that he could argue with "going over his head"---this hints that admirals don't see it as appropriate for peons to contact them directly.  After all, the admiral outright called it a problem.

 > **BASHIR**: Garak, I'm a doctor, not---

Yes, we get it, we all watched the original series...

 > **BASHIR**: It's about Garak, the Cardassian? I'm afraid this relationship has gotten a little out of hand.

C'mon, you haven't even kissed him, doctor...

Slightly more seriously, at this point, the "relationship" actually looks more than a bit like tricking him into shopping at Garak's store, hardly something that has careened out of control.

 > **GARAK**: Quickly, Doctor, you're late. It's twenty fifty-seven. Now just take this and try that on as many times as you like, and stay very, very quiet.
 >
 > **BASHIR**: I was just---

And yes, he thinks that he actually does need the suit, because he checks it out.

 > **TAHNA**: No more wormhole, no more Federation or Cardassians. Or anyone. Drop out of warp. Now!

Did he not watch the pilot episode?  The provisional government asked for the Federation's help administering the station *before* anybody discovered the wormhole.  Shutting it down only resets the clock to before Bajorans had trading partners.

## Conclusions

This went fairly quickly, given that so little of it even included Federation citizens.  But we still managed to squeeze a bit of information out.

### The Bad

Inconsistent with other events, at least Sisko appears to believe that the Bajorans need to agree with his and Starfleet's agenda.  And the Federation may have lost interest in Bajor except for the wormhole.

The Federation apparently believes that all cultures at war commit war crimes or terrorist actions.
