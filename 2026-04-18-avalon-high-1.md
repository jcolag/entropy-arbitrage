## Avalon High

I can't find *anything* about this comic.  Or, rather, I can find a lot about **a** comic [by the same name](https://megcabot.com/series/avalon-high/) by prolific novelist Meg Cabot, but that doesn't help us any.  After some exhaustive searches, I found [an old review {% cc %}](http://comixtalk.com/avalon_josh_phillips_reviewed_apis_teicher/) that claims that the comic holds/held the following description, and tries really hard to tie the name to Arthurian legend.

> This is the story of the students of Avalon High School. It is an exploration of emotion, relationships, and the experiences of youth.

In short, the review praises the writing---especially later in the run---and chokes a bit on the art style, calling it "something of an acquired taste."

Looking specifically for the author's name turned up a [TV Tropes {% cc by-nc-sa %} page](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Webcomic/Avalon1999) with the site's characteristic tedious[^wbB4vm] "things that it does, and also things that it doesn't do, because we want to maximize ad impressions" tone.  It, however, does provide us with a premise of sixteen-year-old Ceilidh MacFarlene's family moving to fictional Avalon, Ontario, befriending some established students.

[^wbB4vm]:  I admit it.  As much as people have talked over the years about the site's addictive nature, I don't think that I've ever managed to endure that tone for anything more than a quick glance.

Anyway, because the comic had near-daily updates for years *and* the hosting site has broken the drop-down list navigation making it difficult to get a sense of the full scope, we'll only look at "Volume 1"---the first two semesters---over three weeks.  I have no idea how many pages it actually runs.

## What Works Well?

Sometimes, the jokes land well.  This one got a laugh out of me.

![A cat approached Ceilidh at a desk, meowing.  She meows back, and they go back and forth as a pseudo-conversation, until someone screams for them to cut it out. Ceilidh blames the cat](/blog/assets/avalon20000121.png "Back when I had a cat, family urged me not to have conversations him in front of guests...")

I would never blame a cat for luring me into a conversation---that [mind-control parasite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toxoplasma_gondii#Behavioral_differences_of_infected_hosts) at work, maybe---but I definitely get the "encourage vocalizing to see if he tries to mimic speech" urge.

## What Works...Less Well?

{% include lesswell.md %}

To get it out of the way, if you check the Mastodon post announcing the new license for the comic, the creator notes the small size---almost difficult to read on modern screens, in fact---and has many "of its time" jokes that I doubt many modern readers would back.  If you think of it as a low-budget sitcom of the era, the inappropriate parts won't seem out of place, even if that doesn't defend them.

## Opportunities

I don't see any interaction remaining, reinforce by the creator giving the comic a public license after a quarter-century, with comments such as "a different time back then, and I was a different person who created something I now find troublesome in places," or more specifically, this.

> ...if anyone wants to see more of it or done better, that can still happen even without me having to be involved.

From the article above, though, I gather that the comic did have an active collaborative community around it.

## What's Adaptable?

Avalon and its high school seem like the headliners, here, surprising absolutely nobody.  And we get a mention that the [Y2K problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Year_2000_problem) only affected some military satellites, an odd choice.
