## What Works Well?

I love the animation style, here, and wish that it had more activity to it so that we could see more.  It shows its range of influences, but also doesn't look like anything else that comes immediately to mind.

Similarly, something that I rarely get to comment on, this has highly effective sound design.  We get a largely quiet soundtrack, but enough noise makes the spaces feel more dynamic than the limited animation does alone.

Likewise, any of the vignettes could tolerate plenty of expansion.  We have a nice mix of genres---showing off that animation style---and they could all support more storytelling.

## What Works...Less Well?

{% include lesswell.md %}

Quite a bit like [**Sol Levante**]({% post_url 2026-01-10-sol-levante %}), it feels like the different scenes should connect more than they do.  Some scenes appear to move to different locations, such as the kid who goes from the train platform to the pond.  Maybe we see the same pond there that the protagonist drew.  And sometimes, it seems like we imagine the imagining of the imagining, but other times the connection doesn't come together.

## Opportunities

I don't see any references to helping push them along, anywhere.  But I assume that kind, non-creepy messages to a young artist wouldn't hurt any.

## What's Adaptable?

As I mentioned, any of the vignettes could stand expansion into stories of their own, though the portal and the flying machines probably stand out the most.
