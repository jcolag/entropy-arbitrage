For those who care, I listened to the soundtrack around when it came out and it didn't really stick with me, largely because the opening song (*Let Mother Vote*) turned me right off[^cj4uDc].  It has a couple of similar pieces, plus one piece (*Find a Way*) that feels almost like a **Hamilton** parody, but the rest of it hits hard, especially after the [*Louisiana v. Callais*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louisiana_v._Callais) decision.  If you like musicals and can't/won't see it in a theater, carve out the two-plus hours.

[^cj4uDc]:  As an analogy in style, imagine if **Wicked** opened with *Wonderful*, expository and relatively bland...

Add in the legitimate history drawn from [**Jailed for Freedom**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jailed_for_Freedom), and I think that it especially works as the counterweight to **Hamilton** that people have talked about needing, not erasing the ugliness in the movement or implying that we solved the problem.
