## FCC Antics

Nothing that I posted---except to boost somebody else's toot---but as this goes out, you still have a few hours to submit a comment on the FCC's propsed rule [classifying media including gender and sexual minorities as "mature"](https://glaad.org/fcc/), proceeding 19-41.  If it helps you craft your comment, I wrote this.

> This directly violates the First Amendment to the Constitution and the principles of freedom of expression that the FCC exists to defend.  Despite what you may have read in the Project 2025 document associating the existence of gender and sexual minorities in media with pornography, nothing about their lives (not already covered by FCC rules) qualifies as "mature content."
>
> Further, relevant research shows that depriving younger people of a diverse set of role-models has a variety of negative health impacts.  And on a personal note, I lived through the weakening of censorship on television during the 1970s, and it resulted in some of the best storytelling that I have seen, a literal win for everybody.
>
> As an educator (among other things), I vehemently oppose all discriminatory labels, and urge you to discard this rule.  Mainstream entertainment shouldn't and can't be forced to cater to the narrowest view of who gets to participate in society.

I've seen research to that effect, haven't I?

Anyway, always go for "polite and helpful," but especially note the final paragraph.  If you have any reason why this issue might have some importance to you in particular---you have children, you have transgender folks in your life, whatever---mention it when you make your point.  For a letter, I'd put it up front, but it flowed better this way for the comment.
